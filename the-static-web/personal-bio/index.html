BLAIR McMILLAN

DRUMMER - 1999-2015

Disc Golf from Joe Canali on Vimeo.

Originally hailing from Scotland, Blair McMillan is an accomplished musician based in Nashville, TN, via Berlin, and Brooklyn.
As a drummer, McMillan has toured and recorded with a broad range of acclaimed artists, such as Findlay Napier, Rachel Sermanni, Daniel Thorpe, Katie Sutherland, Hamish Napier, Hannah Read, The Ansion, Miss Tess & The Talkbacks, Nora Jane Struthers, Rachel Baiman, and Emma Beaton and Nic Gareiss, to name a few.

He has also worked with acclaimed producers, including Steve Chrisanthou (Corinne Bailey Rae), David Kosten (Coldplay, The Flaming Lips, Bat For Lashes), Matty Benbrook (Paulo Nutini, Dido) and Alex James of Blur.

Live, Blair has appeared at a number of major festivals in the U.K and U.S.A including, T in the Park, Rockness, Celtic Connections, Secret Garden Party, Glasgow Jazz Festival, Edinburgh Fringe Festival, Floyd Fest, Telluride Bluegrass Festival, Fibark Festival and New York Tartan Week. He has also shared the stage with artists such as, Sir Elton John, Blondie, Jim Lauderdale, Keane, Candi Staton, McFly and many more, as well as TV and radio appearances worldwide from the BBC TV and radio to Music City Roots for PBS in the United States.

Aside from working with original artists, Blair worked as the principle percussionist onboard the Disney Dream cruise ship for it’s inaugural sailings in 2011. From there, he went onto work with acclaimed theatre show, The 12 Tenors, which toured Europe and China extensively between 2012 and 2013.

Off stage, Blair has a wealth of teaching experience and has taught at summer schools across the U.K as well as teaching a Yamaha Music School U.K and Queen Victoria Military school in Dunblane, Scotland.

Blair currently endorses Duallist drum pedals, Protection Racket cases and the Cajon Brush by Paul Jennings and is available for touring, studio and teaching work.

SONGWRITER - 2013-2015

As a songwriter, Blair McMillan delivers deeply personal vignettes of the world as he views it. Not one to ramble on, his songs cut to the chase and cut to the very core of the listener. His unique ability to create tiny glimmers of humor in even the darkest of song makes for a deeply personal and vulnerable outpouring of lyrically astute songwriting and very real emotion. It’s two chords and the truth type stuff.

In 2014, Blair won the Nairn Book Festival Songwriting Competition judged by accomplished Scottish songwriters Gillian Frame and Findlay Napier. Since arriving in the United States, Blair has appeared as a solo artist on numerous occasions in New York and Nashville as well as appearing at the 2014 Campfire Festival at Club Passim in Cambridge, MA.

In the summer of 2015, Blair toured the west coast of the United States and Canada with David Moss and Emma Beaton in support of his debut release, Religious Song For Non Believers, a collection of original material which McMillan self-released in June 2015.

Blair released his debut album "Religious Songs For Non Believers" at the end of 2015.
